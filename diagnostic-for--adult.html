DIAGNOSTICS FOR ADULT

         The prevalence of hearing loss varies with age, affecting at least 25 percent of patients older than 50 years and more than 50 percent of those older than 80 years. Adolescents and young adults represent groups in which the prevalence of hearing loss is increasing and may therefore benefit from screening. If offered, screening can be performed periodically by asking the patient or family if there are perceived hearing problems, or by using clinical office tests such as whispered voice, finger rub, or audiometry. Audiometry in the family medicine clinic setting is a relatively simple procedure that can be interpreted by a trained health care professional.

 

 

 

What is hearing loss?

 

Hearing loss affects people of all ages. It can vary from profound impairment to treatable ear canal blockages, and its effects can be temporary or permanent. While gradual hearing loss happens over time, some of us are born with congenital or permanent hearing loss.
